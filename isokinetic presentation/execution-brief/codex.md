# Execution Brief  
## Isokinetic 2026 Talk: FIFA Global Burden of Injury and Illness in Football Living Systematic Review  
### Phase 1 Only

## Purpose

This brief translates the approved planning direction into an execution-ready conference structure for the Isokinetic talk. It is focused on Phase 1 only: the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review.

The talk should position the project as living evidence infrastructure for football player health, not as a dashboard presentation and not as a preview of later project phases.

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails

- Stay within Phase 1 throughout.
- Keep the data-status caveat explicit whenever provisional extraction patterns appear.
- Use the 2026 surveillance-map paper as the opening framing reference, not as a parallel review.
- Keep the dashboard clearly secondary to the Living Systematic Review story.
- Avoid product-demo language, software walkthrough logic, or Phase 2/3 forward selling.
- Treat current extraction outputs as provisional descriptive signals only, not final epidemiological findings.

## Core Talk Proposition

Football is global, but its injury and illness evidence base remains fragmented across disciplines, populations, settings, and reporting standards. The FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to convert that fragmented literature into a continuously updated, consensus-aligned evidence system.

## Recommended Talk Shape

Assumption: standard 15 to 20 minute conference slot.

A strong delivery shape is 5 sections across approximately 11 to 13 slides:

1. The problem: global sport, fragmented evidence
2. Why now: the surveillance landscape shows patchy but real activity
3. What FIFA GBI Phase 1 is: the living review as infrastructure
4. Why comparability matters: consensus alignment as the scientific backbone
5. What the current provisional landscape suggests, and why that matters for the field

## Refined Talk Structure

### 1. Opening Problem Frame  
**Goal:** establish urgency before introducing the project.

**Speaking logic:**
- Begin with the scale of football as a truly global sport.
- Contrast that with the fact that player-health evidence is still unevenly captured and unevenly comparable.
- Note that much of the published literature remains concentrated around professional male 11-a-side settings.
- Make clear that the problem is not simply lack of studies, but lack of a living, comparable evidence system.

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 1: Title and one-sentence thesis
- Slide 2: Global sport, fragmented evidence

**What the audience should understand by the end of this section:**
The field has information, but not yet infrastructure.

### 2. Why Now: Surveillance Landscape as Framing Context  
**Goal:** use the surveillance-map paper to justify the need for a living review.

**Speaking logic:**
- Introduce the 2026 surveillance-map paper as the clearest current picture of football health surveillance.
- Use two or three directional points only:
  - surveillance activity exists across many countries
  - programmes remain concentrated in a limited number of contexts
  - injury capture is more common than illness or mental-health capture
  - consensus methodology is not universal
- Pivot quickly from “the landscape exists” to “the landscape is patchy,” which is why synthesis infrastructure is needed.

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 3: Global but uneven surveillance map
- Optional Slide 4: Injury vs illness vs mental-health capture asymmetry

**Execution note:**
This section should stay concise. The paper frames the need; it does not become the talk.

### 3. What FIFA GBI Phase 1 Is  
**Goal:** define the review clearly and make the architecture memorable.

**Speaking logic:**
- Introduce FIFA GBI as a Living Systematic Review, not a one-off review paper.
- State the four football disciplines clearly:
  - association football
  - futsal
  - beach soccer
  - para football
- Emphasize inclusion across sexes, age groups, levels of play, and languages.
- Clarify that the review is designed for annual updates and long-term evidence maintenance.
- Mention the dashboard only as one future dissemination route after the scientific system is understood.

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 5: What FIFA GBI Phase 1 covers
- Slide 6: Living review cycle: search, extract, validate, update, disseminate

**What must land here:**
The novelty is not size alone. The novelty is living, inclusive, continuously updated evidence infrastructure across the full ecology of football.

### 4. Why Comparability Matters  
**Goal:** give the talk its academic backbone without becoming overly technical.

**Speaking logic:**
- Transition from scope to scientific usefulness: breadth only matters if studies can be compared.
- Walk the audience through the consensus lineage:
  - Fuller et al. 2006 for football-specific injury surveillance foundations
  - Bahr et al. 2020 for broader athlete health, burden, and reporting logic
  - Waldén et al. 2023 for football-specific extension and reporting discipline
- Keep the emphasis on applied consequences:
  - clear population description
  - consistent exposure definition
  - distinction between incidence and burden
  - visibility of illness and mental health
  - reporting quality that supports interpretation

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 7: Consensus lineage timeline
- Slide 8: Comparability is what makes living synthesis possible

**Key spoken takeaway:**
The field does not only need more studies. It needs studies that can enter a comparable living evidence system.

### 5. Provisional Descriptive Landscape  
**Goal:** show what the current extraction already reveals without overclaiming.

**Speaking logic:**
- Introduce a clear caveat at the start: the current dataset remains under cleaning and harmonisation.
- Use provisional language consistently:
  - “preliminary extraction patterns suggest”
  - “directional signals in the current dataset”
  - “to be confirmed against the final analysis dataset”
- Show only descriptive coverage patterns that are safe:
  - association football dominates the current evidence base
  - male-coded studies materially outnumber female-coded studies
  - professional and elite settings dominate more than youth and amateur settings
  - injury fields are much more populated than illness fields
- Frame these as signals about the shape of the evidence base, not conclusions about burden itself.

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 9: Preliminary evidence landscape by discipline
- Slide 10: Preliminary imbalance by sex, level, and outcome visibility

**Essential caveat for this section:**
These are provisional descriptive patterns from the current extracted dataset, not final pooled findings and not locked study counts.

### 6. Closing Direction and Practical Value  
**Goal:** leave the audience with relevance, not just information.

**Speaking logic:**
- Reassert that FIFA GBI is building a living evidence system for football.
- State that annual updates are planned.
- Briefly note that dissemination outputs may include a FIFA-hosted dashboard and public reporting, but keep this secondary.
- End with a field-facing message:
  - if clinicians and researchers want their work to inform the evolving evidence base, consensus-aligned definitions and complete reporting are essential

**Recommended slide group:**
- Slide 11: Living review as ongoing infrastructure
- Slide 12: Closing takeaway

## Section-by-Section Speaking Logic Summary

### Opening
Start with the mismatch between the scale of football and the fragmentation of the evidence base.

### Framing reference
Use the surveillance-map paper to show that surveillance activity exists, but is uneven in geography, competition type, and outcome capture.

### Project definition
Present FIFA GBI as the response: an inclusive, living systematic review built for updating and comparability.

### Scientific logic
Explain that consensus alignment is what allows the review to function as evidence infrastructure rather than as a large but messy archive.

### Provisional evidence shape
Show what the current extraction suggests about concentration and underrepresentation, while keeping the provisional caveat explicit.

### Closing
Return to audience value: comparable reporting and better-designed surveillance are how the field contributes to a living evidence system.

## Visual Direction

Visual treatment should remain restrained, academic, and conference-appropriate.

### Style
- White or very light background
- Dark text
- FIFA-blue accent used sparingly
- Large titles and short declarative headlines
- Minimal chart furniture
- One idea per slide

### Visual emphasis
- Prefer maps, timelines, clean bar charts, dot plots, and simple process diagrams
- Avoid screenshots of the extraction environment or dashboard UI in the main talk body
- Add a subtle but persistent “Preliminary data” label on all results-oriented slides
- Use subtitles to reinforce caveats without overloading narration

### Tone
The deck should look like a rigorous keynote, not a product pitch and not a startup demo.

## Remotion Scene Opportunities

Remotion should support clarity, grouping, and pacing rather than spectacle.

### 1. Fragmentation to system
Animated scattered study nodes or categories resolve into one coherent living-review structure.  
Use for the opening transition from problem to solution.

### 2. Global but uneven
A world map with dots appearing by region, showing concentration rather than universal spread.  
Use while discussing the surveillance-map framing paper.

### 3. Scope expansion
Reveal the four football disciplines one by one, then layer on sex, age, level, and language dimensions.  
Use to show why the review’s scope is structurally different from prior reviews.

### 4. Consensus lineage
A minimal timeline animates from 2006 to 2020 to 2023, with a short label beneath each milestone.  
Use to reinforce that comparability is constructed through reporting standards.

### 5. Preliminary pattern reveal
Bars or dots draw on sequentially for discipline concentration, sex imbalance, and injury-versus-illness reporting asymmetry.  
Keep motion calm and linear so the visuals do not imply stronger inference than the data supports.

### 6. Living update loop
A circular workflow animates through search, extract, validate, update, and disseminate.  
Use near the end to reinforce that this is recurring infrastructure.

### 7. Quiet dissemination endpoint
If included at all, dashboard or report outputs appear only in the closing sequence, with understated fade-in.  
This keeps the review story primary and the dissemination story secondary.

## Provisional Data Language to Use Verbatim

Use language of this kind during the descriptive section:

- “These are preliminary extraction patterns from the current dataset.”
- “The dataset remains under cleaning and harmonisation.”
- “These are directional signals rather than final epidemiological estimates.”
- “All exact figures will need confirmation against the final analysis dataset.”

Avoid:
- “We found that”
- “The evidence shows”
- any locked percentage or pooled claim not yet validated

## Closing Takeaway

A strong closing sentence for the final slide:

**Football does not only need more player-health studies. It needs comparable, living evidence infrastructure that can keep pace with the game.**

## Audience Value

By the end of the talk, the audience should leave with five clear gains:

- a sharper understanding of why football epidemiology still struggles with fragmentation
- a clear picture of what the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to do
- a practical appreciation of why consensus-aligned definitions and reporting matter
- an early view of where the current evidence base remains concentrated or thin, presented with appropriate caution
- a direct implication for their own work: surveillance and cohort studies become more useful when they are designed to be comparable and capable of entering a living evidence system

## Final Recommendation

Build the talk as a scientific argument, not as a project update. Open with fragmentation, use the surveillance-map paper to establish urgency, introduce FIFA GBI Phase 1 as living evidence infrastructure, show why consensus alignment makes that infrastructure credible, and use provisional descriptive patterns only to illustrate the current shape of the evidence base.

If that order is preserved, the talk will feel rigorous, timely, and useful, while keeping the dashboard in its proper place: downstream of the Living Systematic Review, not the story itself.
