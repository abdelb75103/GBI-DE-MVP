## Core Talk Proposition

Football is global, but its injury and illness evidence base remains fragmented across disciplines, populations, settings, and reporting standards. The FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to convert that fragmented literature into a continuously updated, consensus-aligned evidence system.

## 2. Section-by-Section Speaking Logic

### Section A  -  The Global Game, the Fragmented Evidence

**Purpose:** Build tension before introducing the solution. The audience should feel the mismatch between football's global scale and its evidence base before hearing about the project.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Open with the premise: football is the world's most widely played sport. Its player-health evidence should reflect that reach. It does not  -  yet.
2. Name the structural gaps: prior reviews have tended to centre on professional male 11-a-side cohorts. Futsal, beach soccer, para football, women's football, younger age groups, and non-English-language studies remain underrepresented. Static reviews age quickly in a fast-moving surveillance field.
3. Introduce the surveillance-map paper (McCall et al. 2026) as the best current picture of global surveillance activity. Use two or three directional findings:
   - 49 ongoing national-league surveillance programmes identified across 53 countries  -  real activity, but concentrated in a limited number of nations and predominantly in men's competitions.
   - Illness and mental-health capture are less common than injury capture across existing programmes.
   - Consensus-based methodology is not universally adopted.
4. Land the scene-setting conclusion: the surveillance infrastructure exists but is patchy and inconsistent. Static reviews cannot keep up. This is the gap that motivates what follows.

**Tone:** Measured, evidence-grounded, not dramatic. The problem is structural, not scandalous.

**Surveillance-map paper boundary:** Present it as scene-setting only. Do not reproduce its full results or turn this section into a standalone surveillance-programme review. The paper frames the need; the living review is the response.

**What the audience should understand by the end of this section:** The field has information, but not yet infrastructure.

---

### Section B  -  What FIFA GBI Phase 1 Is

**Purpose:** Shift from problem to response. The audience should understand the living review as a designed system, not a conventional one-off publication.

**Speaking logic:**

1. State the review's inclusive scope: four football disciplines (association football, futsal, beach soccer, para football), all sexes, age groups, competitive levels, and languages. Prospective cohort studies and randomised trials reporting injury, illness, or mental health in competitive football settings.
2. Explain the living architecture: this is not a single systematic review. It is designed for structured extraction, quality control, and annual updates. The evidence base grows; the synthesis stays current.
3. Briefly name the dissemination pathway: a FIFA-hosted dashboard and a public report aligned with major tournament cycles. These are outputs of the review, not the subject of the talk. Mention them to show practical intent, then move on.
4. Anchor the novelty claim: FIFA GBI is novel not because it is larger than prior reviews, but because it is designed as living evidence infrastructure for the full ecology of football.

**Tone:** Confident but not promotional. This is a scientific architecture, not a product.

**Dashboard boundary:** One sentence maximum. Do not show the dashboard here. Do not describe its features.

**What the audience should understand by the end of this section:** The novelty is not size alone  -  it is living, inclusive, continuously updated evidence infrastructure across the full ecology of football.

---

### Section C  -  Comparability and the Consensus Backbone

**Purpose:** Carry the methodological weight of the talk. The audience should understand that the living review's value depends on whether the studies it absorbs can be compared.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Open with the core message: more studies alone will not solve the problem. Better classification, exposure capture, and reporting discipline matter just as much.
2. Walk through the consensus lineage as a three-node sequence:
   - **Fuller et al. 2006**  -  established the football-specific baseline: injury definitions, severity, recurrence, exposure capture.
   - **Bahr et al. 2020 (IOC consensus / STROBE-SIIS)**  -  widened the lens to athlete health problems broadly, with explicit guidance on severity, burden, population description, and reporting standards.
   - **Waldén et al. 2023**  -  translated the broader IOC framework back into football practice, reinforcing player-level exposure capture and sport-specific diagnostic coding.
3. Connect the lineage to the living review: this consensus sequence is not background decoration. It is the backbone that makes extraction comparable and future synthesis defensible.
4. Name the priority emphasis areas without over-indexing on technical detail:
   - Population description: discipline, sex, age, level, setting
   - Exposure definition and measurement
   - The distinction between incidence and burden
   - Illness and mental-health visibility alongside injury
   - Reporting quality that supports interpretation
5. Land the section summary: a living review only becomes useful if the studies it absorbs can be interpreted through comparable definitions, exposure logic, and burden reporting frameworks.

**Tone:** Intellectually serious. This section earns credibility with the academic portion of the audience. Keep it clear, not dense.

**Key spoken takeaway:** The field does not only need more studies. It needs studies that can enter a comparable living evidence system.

---

### Section D  -  The Preliminary Landscape

**Purpose:** Show what the living review is already capturing and where the evidence concentrates and thins out. This section must be explicitly marked as provisional throughout.

**Data-status caveat  -  introduce once, reinforce visually:**

At the top of this section, state clearly: the patterns shown here are drawn from the current extraction dataset, which remains under cleaning and harmonisation. They are directional signals, not final epidemiological results. All figures will be confirmed against the final analysis dataset before publication.

After this verbal introduction, a persistent "Preliminary data" footer label on every results-oriented slide handles ongoing signalling without constant spoken disclaimers that break the flow.

**Speaking logic:**

1. **Discipline coverage.** The evidence base is overwhelmingly association football. Futsal, para football, and beach soccer are represented but at much smaller scale. This is a gap the living review is designed to track over time.
2. **Sex distribution.** Male-coded studies materially outnumber female-coded studies. This is a known structural imbalance, not a surprise  -  but a living review makes the imbalance measurable and trackable.
3. **Level of play.** Professional and elite settings dominate. Amateur and youth representation is thinner. The living review captures the level variable explicitly, so this gap becomes visible rather than assumed.
4. **Outcome availability.** Injury fields are far more populated than illness fields. Mental-health capture is sparser still. This mirrors the surveillance-map paper's finding that illness and mental-health capture lag behind injury capture in existing programmes.
5. **Temporal distribution.** Reasonable representation from 2020-2024, indicating active research production and a live pipeline of eligible studies.
6. Close this section by reframing the gaps as the argument for a living system: these patterns show where the evidence concentrates. A living review is the instrument that lets us track whether and how those gaps close with each annual update.

**Claims this section must not make:**

- Final pooled incidence or burden estimates
- Definitive comparisons between football disciplines
- Locked study counts
- Strong illness or mental-health prevalence conclusions
- Any suggestion that the provisional export equals the final analysis dataset

---

### Section E  -  Forward Direction and Practical Takeaway

**Purpose:** Close with forward momentum and a concrete call to action. The audience should leave knowing what happens next and what they can do.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Name the forward path briefly:
   - Annual living-review updates
   - A FIFA-hosted dashboard as a dissemination output
   - A public report aligned with major tournament cycles
   - The evidence system designed for use by clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers
2. Deliver the practical takeaway directly:

   > **If you run a surveillance programme, design a cohort study, or report football injury and illness data  -  consensus-aligned definitions and complete reporting are what allow your work to enter a living evidence system. The field needs your data, but it needs your data to be comparable.**

3. Reinforce what the audience can act on:
   - Design surveillance studies so they can be compared across contexts
   - Report exposure, definitions, burden, and population characteristics clearly
   - Resist the assumption that evidence from professional men's 11-a-side football generalises to the whole sport
   - Treat living synthesis as part of player-health infrastructure, not an academic abstraction

**Tone:** Direct, collegial, forward-looking. Not a sales pitch. Not a plea. A professional statement of shared responsibility.

## 4. Visual Direction

### Design Language

- **Background:** White or very light neutral (off-white, warm grey at most).
- **Text:** Dark grey or near-black for body text. High contrast.
- **Accent colour:** FIFA blue as the primary accent. Use sparingly  -  headings, chart fills, key-sentence highlights.
- **Typography:** Large, confident type for key claims. Sans-serif throughout. Minimum readable body size on screen: 24pt equivalent.
- **One idea per slide.** No composite layouts with competing visuals.
- **Academic keynote aesthetic.** Clean, restrained, high signal-to-noise. No gradients, drop shadows, or decorative elements.

### Chart and Data Visual Rules

- Bar charts: horizontal, single-colour fill (FIFA blue), labelled directly (no legends if avoidable).
- Proportional charts: waffle or area, not pie charts.
- All provisional-data slides carry a persistent "Preliminary data" footer label in a muted but readable weight.
- No decimals in any provisional figure. Round to whole numbers or use qualitative descriptors ("materially outnumber," "much smaller representation").
- No confidence intervals, p-values, or forest plots. This is descriptive landscape, not meta-analysis.

### What Not To Show

- No screenshots of the extraction tool or dashboard UI in the main talk body.
- No software architecture diagrams.
- No Phase 2 or Phase 3 material.
- No dashboard walkthrough slides.

## 5. Remotion Scene Opportunities

Each scene is designed for progressive reveal and structural clarity, not decoration. Motion should slow down for key claims and accelerate slightly during transitional passages.

### Scene 1  -  Fragmentation to System (Section A → B transition)

Scattered elements  -  study icons, discipline labels, population tags  -  float on screen in a loose, unstructured composition. On cue, they animate into a structured arrangement: the living-review architecture diagram. The transition takes 3-4 seconds.

**Message:** The project brings order to a fragmented evidence base.
**Technique:** Opacity fade-in for scattered elements, then coordinated translate and scale to final positions. Ease-out timing.

### Scene 2  -  Global but Uneven (Slide A2)

A world map or distributed-node composition. Dots animate in by region, staggered over 2-3 seconds. Concentration in certain areas becomes immediately visible; blank zones stay empty.

**Message:** Football is global, but surveillance and evidence are not evenly distributed.
**Technique:** Staggered opacity + scale-up per dot, grouped by region. No bounce or overshoot  -  clean appear.

### Scene 3  -  Scope Expansion (Slide B1)

The four football discipline icons appear sequentially (association football, futsal, beach soccer, para football). After all four are visible, subgroup dimensions  -  sex, age, level, language  -  radiate outward from the centre as labelled lines or nodes.

**Message:** Novelty comes from inclusive scope, not study volume alone.
**Technique:** Sequential fade-in for disciplines (0.5s stagger), then radial draw-on for subgroup dimensions.

### Scene 4  -  Consensus as Backbone (Slide C2)

Three timeline nodes (2006, 2020, 2023) appear in sequence. Each node expands briefly to reveal its one-line contribution, then contracts to its compact label. The timeline line draws on left-to-right connecting them.

**Message:** Comparability rests on a deliberate scientific lineage.
**Technique:** Draw-on line, sequential node expand/contract, text fade-in.

### Scene 5  -  Preliminary Pattern Reveal (Slides D2-D5)

Charts build sequentially within each slide. Bars draw on from left to right (horizontal bar charts) or bottom to top. Fills appear progressively rather than all at once. The "Preliminary data" footer is persistent, fading in once at D1 and remaining.

**Message:** The current landscape already shows where evidence concentrates and where it thins out.
**Technique:** Draw-on bar fills with staggered timing. Opacity for labels. No morphing between charts  -  each chart is its own composition.

### Scene 6  -  Living Update Loop (Slide B2)

The circular process diagram (search → extract → validate → update → disseminate) animates as a draw-on loop. Each node highlights in sequence, with a brief label appear. The loop completes and pulses once to reinforce the recurring nature.

**Message:** This is recurring infrastructure, not a static publication.
**Technique:** Circular path draw-on, sequential node highlight, single pulse at completion.

### Scene 7  -  End-State Utility (Slide E1)

Dashboard and report outputs appear last in the roadmap, with a quiet sequential fade-in. They arrive after the annual-update node, reinforcing that dissemination follows the science.

**Message:** Dissemination follows the science; it does not replace it.
**Technique:** Sequential fade-in, 0.8s stagger, no emphasis animation. Understated by design.

### Animation Principles (All Scenes)

- Transitions: horizontal wipes, opacity fades, draw-on effects. No 3D, no bounces, no rapid morphs.
- Tempo: slower for key claims (Scenes 1, 4), moderate for data (Scene 5), quick for structural/transitional moments (Scenes 6, 7).
- No animation should imply stronger inference than the data supports. Charts should build, not transform.

## 6. Provisional Data Language Guide

### Use verbatim or in close paraphrase during Section D:

- "These are preliminary extraction patterns from the current dataset."
- "The dataset remains under cleaning and harmonisation."
- "These are directional signals rather than final epidemiological estimates."
- "All exact figures will need confirmation against the final analysis dataset."
- "These patterns will be confirmed against the final analysis dataset."

### Avoid:

- "We found that…" (implies completed analysis)
- "The evidence shows…" (implies synthesis-level confidence)
- "X% of studies report…" (implies locked denominators)
- Any locked percentage, decimal, confidence interval, or pooled claim not yet validated

## 7. Closing Takeaway and Audience Value

### What the audience should remember

Three things, in priority order:

1. **The problem is real and current.** Football epidemiology evidence is fragmented across disciplines, populations, and reporting standards. Existing surveillance is globally uneven.

2. **FIFA GBI is building a living system, not publishing a single paper.** The living review architecture  -  consensus-aligned extraction, structured quality control, annual updates, accessible dissemination  -  is designed to keep the evidence base current and comparable.

3. **Their own work can contribute.** If they collect or report football health data, consensus-aligned definitions and complete reporting are what allow that work to enter a living evidence system. The field needs comparable data, not just more of it.

### Five gains the audience takes away

By the end of the talk, the audience should leave with:

- A sharper understanding of why football epidemiology still struggles with fragmentation
- A clear picture of what the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to do
- A practical appreciation of why consensus-aligned definitions and reporting matter
- An early view of where the current evidence base remains concentrated or thin, presented with appropriate caution
- A direct implication for their own work: surveillance and cohort studies become more useful when they are designed to be comparable and capable of entering a living evidence system

### The single closing sentence

> **The field needs your data, but it needs your data to be comparable.**

This sentence should appear as a full-slide typographic treatment on the final content slide before acknowledgments. It is the line the audience takes home.
