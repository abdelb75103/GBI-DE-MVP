# Execution Brief

## Working Assumption

Assume a 12 to 15 minute conference talk. If the slot is shorter, merge slide groups 4 and 5 before cutting the opening or closing logic.

## Presentation Thesis

Football has a global player-health problem but still lacks a truly global, comparable, continuously updated evidence base. FIFA GBI's Phase 1 living systematic review is being built to fill that gap, and the current provisional extraction dataset already shows both the opportunity and the blind spots.

## Refined Talk Structure

### Slide Group 1: Why the field still has an evidence problem

Purpose:
Set up the mismatch between the global game and the fragmented evidence base.

Speaking logic:
Open on scale and fragmentation. Make the audience feel that football epidemiology is still too narrow, too static, and too difficult to compare across settings. Use the 2026 surveillance-map paper as proof that surveillance activity exists but remains uneven.

Visual direction:
Minimal world map or network view with uneven coverage, not a dense stats slide.

Remotion opportunity:
Sparse nodes appear across the map, then pause before full connection to emphasize incompleteness rather than triumph.

### Slide Group 2: What FIFA GBI is actually building

Purpose:
Introduce FIFA GBI as the Phase 1 response to that evidence gap.

Speaking logic:
Define the project as a living systematic review across association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football, covering sexes, ages, levels, and languages. Keep the phrasing disciplined: this is evidence infrastructure, not just another review.

Visual direction:
One clean architecture slide with four disciplines, broad inclusion categories, and the living-update model.

Remotion opportunity:
Single-sport frame expands into the four-discipline system, then into sex, age, and level layers.

### Slide Group 3: Why FIFA GBI is different from earlier reviews

Purpose:
Show novelty without sounding promotional.

Speaking logic:
Move from "broader scope" to "better long-run utility." Stress that novelty comes from combining global scope, living updates, and consensus-aligned extraction. Mention the future dashboard only as a dissemination route.

Visual direction:
A contrast slide: static, narrow, one-off review model on one side; living, inclusive, updateable review model on the other.

Remotion opportunity:
A static block fades while a living timeline continues forward.

### Slide Group 4: What the provisional export already suggests

Purpose:
Use current data to reveal the shape of the evidence base without overclaiming results.

Speaking logic:
State the caveat first. Then show that the current export is dominated by association football, with much thinner futsal, beach soccer, and para-football representation; male and professional or elite settings remain more common than female or other groups; injury fields are more populated than illness fields. Present these as descriptive signals about coverage and reporting, not final burden estimates.

Visual direction:
Simple coverage bars or small multiples. Avoid polished epidemiological dashboards here.

Remotion opportunity:
Bars or tiles populate unevenly so the imbalance becomes visible before any labels are read.

### Slide Group 5: Why comparability is the real bottleneck

Purpose:
Translate the provisional-data section into a methodological insight.

Speaking logic:
Explain that more studies alone are not enough. Comparability depends on aligned definitions, exposure capture, and reporting choices. Use the 2006 football consensus, the 2020 IOC consensus, and the 2023 football-specific extension as a sequence that moves the field from isolated surveillance toward comparable evidence.

Visual direction:
A clean methodological ladder or framework slide: definition, exposure, incidence, burden, coding, comparability.

Remotion opportunity:
Each layer locks into place, ending in a stable "comparable evidence" frame.

### Slide Group 6: What this enables for the field

Purpose:
Bring the method story back to audience value.

Speaking logic:
Show that a living, consensus-aligned review can support clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers by making it easier to understand where evidence is strong, where it is weak, and where football-specific reporting still needs improvement.

Visual direction:
One audience-value slide with three lanes: clinical, research, decision-making.

Remotion opportunity:
A single evidence stream branches cleanly into the three user groups.

### Slide Group 7: Closing message and future-facing output

Purpose:
End on usefulness, not on features.

Speaking logic:
Reinforce that the goal is a living reference point for football player health. Mention the FIFA-hosted dashboard late and briefly as one dissemination output. The final sentence should return to the central claim: football needs living, globally inclusive, consensus-aligned evidence infrastructure.

Visual direction:
Quiet closing slide. One statement, one supporting visual, no clutter.

Remotion opportunity:
Map, disciplines, and method layers resolve into one stable end frame.

## Section-by-Section Speaking Logic

### Opening

Say:
Football is global, but the evidence we rely on to understand injury and illness still remains fragmented and uneven.

Do:
Lead with the problem and the gap. Do not introduce the dashboard here.

### Project Introduction

Say:
FIFA GBI is building a living systematic review designed to keep integrating evidence across football disciplines, player groups, and time.

Do:
Emphasize Phase 1 only.

### Provisional Findings

Say:
These are early descriptive signals from the current extraction export, not final cleaned-analysis results.

Do:
Use provisional data to show literature shape, reporting density, and subgroup imbalance.

### Consensus And Comparability

Say:
The reason consensus alignment matters is that a living review is only as strong as the comparability of the studies it integrates.

Do:
Keep the method section crisp and practical.

### Close

Say:
If football wants evidence that stays useful, it needs a living system, not another static snapshot.

Do:
End with audience value and long-run field impact.

## Recommended Slide Progression

1. Global game, fragmented evidence
2. Surveillance exists but is uneven
3. FIFA GBI Phase 1: living systematic review
4. What makes FIFA GBI different
5. Provisional dataset caveat
6. Early descriptive signals from the export
7. Why consensus-aligned reporting matters
8. What a living evidence system enables
9. Dissemination route, including FIFA-hosted dashboard
10. Closing takeaway

## Specific But Light Visual Direction

- Keep typography restrained and conference-clean.
- Favor white or off-white backgrounds with strong contrast and disciplined accent color.
- Use maps, structured diagrams, and simple bars rather than app-like UI.
- Avoid purple-heavy startup styling or glossy product aesthetics.
- Use animation only when it clarifies progression: fragmentation to system, single-discipline to multi-discipline, raw extraction to comparable evidence.

## Remotion Scene Opportunities

- Opening map with uneven surveillance distribution.
- Four-discipline expansion sequence.
- Evidence-pipeline scene from study extraction to living review to dissemination.
- Coverage-gap scene showing where illness, mental health, or underrepresented football contexts remain thin.
- Closing scene where fragmented evidence resolves into one living system.

## Closing Takeaway

The audience should leave with this message: FIFA GBI is not just compiling football injury and illness studies. It is building the living evidence backbone the field needs in order to compare, update, and act on player-health knowledge more responsibly.

## Non-Negotiables During Slide Build

- Keep every results-style slide explicitly marked as preliminary if it uses the current export.
- Keep the dashboard secondary and late.
- Keep the surveillance-map paper as framing reference, not as the main topic.
- Do not drift into Phase 2, Phase 3, or product-demo language.
