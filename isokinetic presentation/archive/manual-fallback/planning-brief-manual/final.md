# Planning Brief

## Core Recommendation

The talk should argue that football does not just need another review. It needs a living, globally inclusive evidence system. FIFA GBI matters because it is being built to turn fragmented surveillance studies into a continuously updated reference point across football disciplines, sexes, ages, levels of play, and languages.

That is the strongest story because it connects the audience's existing frustration with fragmented epidemiology to a concrete Phase 1 solution. It also lets the provisional data work for the talk without forcing premature pooled claims.

## Thesis

Football is the world's game, but football player-health evidence is still narrow, uneven, and often hard to compare. FIFA GBI is building a living systematic review that can close those gaps by combining global scope, consensus-aligned extraction, and ongoing evidence updates. The current provisional dataset already shows why that work is needed.

## Why This Narrative Wins

- It matches the local project documents, the Isokinetic abstract, and the conference framing.
- It uses the 2026 surveillance-map paper as a scene-setter rather than a competing storyline.
- It gives the audience a clear problem-solution arc.
- It keeps the dashboard in its proper place as a dissemination output.
- It allows provisional findings to appear as evidence of feasibility and evidence-base shape, not as overclaimed final results.

## Recommended Story Arc

### 1. Start with the evidence problem, not with the project logo

Open by making the tension explicit: football is global, but the epidemiology evidence base is not. Most reviews are static, narrow, and still overrepresent professional male 11-a-side settings. The audience should feel the gap before they hear the project name.

Use the 2026 surveillance-map paper here. Its value is that it shows global surveillance activity exists, but is uneven across countries, confederations, sexes, and outcome types. That makes the need for a living synthesis system obvious.

### 2. Introduce FIFA GBI as infrastructure

Once the problem is established, present FIFA GBI as the Phase 1 response: a living systematic review across association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football, covering all sexes, ages, levels, and languages.

The introduction should emphasize:

- global inclusion
- living updates rather than static review cycles
- consensus-aligned extraction and reporting logic
- eventual synthesis across heterogeneous subgroups

The tone should be: this is a methodologically serious evidence system being built for long-term use.

### 3. Show what makes the project different

The novelty is not just scale. It is the combination of scope, update model, and reporting discipline.

The talk should make clear that FIFA GBI is different because it:

- spans multiple FIFA disciplines instead of stopping at conventional 11-a-side cohorts
- includes underrepresented groups rather than defaulting to adult professional men
- treats languages and global coverage as part of the design, not an afterthought
- builds toward a living evidence resource instead of a one-off publication
- uses consensus-aligned extraction so future subgroup comparisons are more defensible

### 4. Use provisional findings to reveal the shape of the evidence base

This section should not try to be the capstone paper early. Its job is to show what the current extraction export is already revealing about the literature landscape.

The safest provisional messages are:

- the current extract is heavily weighted toward association football
- male and professional or elite settings still dominate the visible evidence base
- injury variables are far more populated than illness variables
- the data structure already makes subgroup reporting and reporting-quality discussion possible

In other words, provisional data should show the audience both the opportunity and the imbalance.

### 5. Turn to comparability and consensus

After showing the provisional data, explain why global scope alone is not enough. Comparability is the real bottleneck.

This is where the 2006 football consensus, the 2020 IOC consensus, and the 2023 football-specific extension become central. The audience should leave understanding that:

- inconsistent definitions weaken comparison
- weak exposure reporting distorts incidence interpretation
- burden and incidence answer different questions
- illness and mental-health surveillance are often underdeveloped

The method story should support the main narrative, not overtake it. Keep it crisp, consequential, and tied to why the living review will be more useful than older static reviews.

### 6. End with value, not software

Close by showing where this is going: a living reference point that helps clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers understand where the literature is strong, where it is thin, and how the field can improve comparability over time.

The FIFA-hosted dashboard should appear here as one route for dissemination and use. It is evidence that the project is designed to travel beyond a paper, but it should not become a product demo.

## Section Balance

Assuming a 12 to 15 minute conference talk, the cleanest balance is:

- 20% on the problem and surveillance landscape
- 25% on FIFA GBI and what makes it different
- 25% on provisional descriptive signals from the current dataset
- 20% on consensus alignment and comparability
- 10% on the practical takeaway and future-facing dissemination

If the slot is shorter, compress the provisional findings before cutting the framing or the comparability logic.

## What To Say About Provisional Findings

Use language such as:

- "current extraction export"
- "early descriptive signal"
- "directional pattern"
- "preliminary until the cleaned analysis dataset is confirmed"

Avoid language such as:

- "global estimate"
- "final burden"
- "definitive comparison"
- "pooled result"

## Visual Direction

The visuals should feel academic, clean, and globally oriented rather than product-led.

Recommended visual lanes:

- world-map or network framing for the opening problem
- a four-discipline organizing frame for novelty
- restrained evidence-landscape charts for provisional findings
- a simple method ladder showing definition, exposure, incidence, burden, and comparability
- one late, quiet glimpse of dissemination outputs rather than a dashboard tour

## Remotion-Friendly Beats

- An opening world map with sparse surveillance nodes that gradually connect into a fuller evidence network.
- A discipline expansion sequence that moves from a single 11-a-side frame into association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football.
- A provisional-data sequence where visible coverage clusters reveal where the literature is dense and where it is thin.
- A comparability sequence that layers definitions, exposure, and burden reporting into one aligned framework.

## Practical Takeaway For The Audience

The audience should leave with one clear idea: if football wants truly useful epidemiology, it needs living, globally inclusive, consensus-aligned evidence systems rather than isolated static reviews. FIFA GBI is being built as that system.

## Guardrails

- Stay within Phase 1 only.
- Keep the provisional-data caveat explicit.
- Do not let the dashboard overtake the science.
- Do not present the current CSV as a cleaned analysis sheet.
- Do not drift into Phase 2 or Phase 3 problem statements.
