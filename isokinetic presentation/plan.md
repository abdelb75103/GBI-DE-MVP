# Plan

## Goal

Produce a conference-talk planning brief for the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review using a research-first Gotenks workflow.

## Scope

- Phase 1 only: the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review
- Exclude Phase 2 and Phase 3 from the talk body
- Use the current CSV only as a directional signal source, not final analysis truth
- Use the 2026 surveillance-map paper as a required framing source

## Workflow

1. Build the research layer.
2. Use the research layer plus local project documents in the first Gotenks run.
3. Treat the first Gotenks `final.md` as the source of truth for the real talk plan.
4. Use that first Gotenks output as an input to the second Gotenks run.
5. Treat the second Gotenks output as the execution brief for slide design, speaker notes, and Remotion planning.

## Non-Negotiables

- Do not lock slide order before the first Gotenks run.
- Keep the talk academic, professional, and memorable.
- The dashboard is a dissemination output of the living review, not the main subject of the talk.
- The story should combine:
  - why the living review exists
  - what makes FIFA GBI different
  - what the provisional data suggest so far
  - why consensus-aligned reporting matters for comparability
- Keep returning to the project's multi-part value proposition:
  - it is the largest systematic review of its kind in this space
  - it is living and designed to update rather than expire
  - it functions as a centralised knowledge base for the global burden of injury and illness in football
  - it turns scattered football health evidence into standardised, comparable, continuously updating evidence
  - once structured centrally, it supports estimation across different sexes, ages, places, and over time where appropriate, with explicit uncertainty
  - it creates the conditions for stronger Bayesian / hierarchical modelling in sparse settings, with explicit uncertainty
- Every slide should serve a clear narrative purpose and connect back to the value of FIFA GBI as evidence infrastructure.
- Avoid disconnected facts or interesting-but-nonessential information that does not advance the argument.
- Preliminary evidence should be used to support that value proposition, not to distract from it.
- Use European / Irish-English spelling consistently across the deck, notes, prompts, and planning material.

## Deliverables

- `research.md`
- first Gotenks planning brief
- second Gotenks execution brief

Only after those exist:

- slide outline
- speaker notes
- Remotion scene plan
