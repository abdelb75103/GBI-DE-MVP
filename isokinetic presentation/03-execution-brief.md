# Execution Brief  -  FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review Conference Talk

## Isokinetic 2026  -  Phase 1 Only

---

## Preamble

This brief translates the approved planning brief into build-ready instructions for the Isokinetic conference talk on Phase 1 of the FIFA Global Burden of Injury and Illness in Football: the Living Systematic Review. It covers talk structure, section-by-section speaking logic, slide progression, visual direction, Remotion scene opportunities, and the closing takeaway.

Everything here stays within Phase 1. Provisional-data caveats are embedded structurally  -  not bolted on as afterthoughts.

---

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails

- Stay within Phase 1 throughout.
- Make the data-status caveat explicit whenever provisional extraction patterns appear.
- Use the 2026 surveillance-map paper to open and frame  -  not as a parallel review in its own right.
- Keep the dashboard clearly secondary to the Living Systematic Review narrative.
- No product-demo language, software walkthrough logic, or Phase 2/3 forward selling.
- Treat current extraction outputs as provisional descriptive signals only, never as final epidemiological findings.
- Keep a visible value-throughline from problem to response to practical utility.
- Every slide must do one of three jobs: define the problem, explain why comparability matters, or make the value of FIFA GBI more concrete.
- Do not keep standalone facts, interesting side notes, or descriptive results that do not clearly set up the next slide or strengthen the central argument.
- Keep reinforcing that FIFA GBI is not just another review, but a large-scale, living, centralised evidence system for the global burden of injury and illness in football.
- When useful, it can be described as the biggest systematic review of its kind, but only to clarify scale and value rather than as empty promotion.
- Treat Bayesian / hierarchical modelling as an important downstream capability this system enables, not as the only value claim.
- Use preliminary evidence to show why this system matters and what it makes visible; do not let preliminary figures become a detached mini-project inside the talk.
- A preferred value line is: "It turns scattered football health evidence into standardised, comparable, continuously updating evidence."
- When discussing enabled analytics, note that a centralised living system can support estimation across sexes, ages, places, and, where appropriate, over time, with explicit uncertainty.
- Any slide that uses a paper's quote, figure, or numerical result must include a clear bottom-of-slide reference to that paper.
- Citation treatment should be elegant and visually integrated into the footer area, but still readable in a large room.
- Add a bibliography / references slide at the end if the deck needs a cleaner place for full citations.
- Use European / Irish-English spelling throughout slide copy, prompts, notes, and speaker material.
- Prefer `s` spellings rather than `z` spellings where applicable, for example `standardised`, `centralised`, `harmonisation`, and `visualisation`.

## Core Talk Proposition

Football is global, but its injury and illness evidence base remains fragmented across disciplines, populations, settings, and reporting standards. The FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to convert that fragmented literature into a continuously updated, consensus-aligned evidence system.

## 2. Section-by-Section Speaking Logic

### Section A  -  The Global Game, the Fragmented Evidence

**Purpose:** Build tension before introducing the solution. The audience should feel the mismatch between football's global scale and its evidence base before they hear a word about the project.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Open with the premise: football is the world's most widely played sport. Its player-health evidence should reflect that reach  -  and it does not. Not yet.
2. Name the structural gaps directly. Prior reviews have tended to centre on professional male 11-a-side cohorts. Futsal, beach soccer, para football, women's football, younger age groups, and non-English-language studies remain underrepresented. And static reviews age quickly in a fast-moving surveillance field.
3. Bring in the surveillance-map paper (McCall et al. 2026) as the best current picture of global surveillance activity. Draw on two or three directional findings:
   - 49 ongoing national-league surveillance programmes identified across 53 countries  -  real activity, but concentrated in a handful of nations and predominantly in men's competitions.
   - Illness and mental-health capture are less common than injury capture across existing programmes.
   - Consensus-based methodology is not universally adopted.
4. If the current export is shown in this section, use it only to show that the same structural concentration appears again in the prospective literature. Do not introduce it as a separate unrelated story.
5. Land the scene-setting conclusion: the field has surveillance activity and literature, but not yet a sufficiently integrated evidence infrastructure. Static reviews cannot keep pace. That gap is what motivates everything that follows.
6. Use the handoff into the next section to make the payoff explicit: FIFA GBI is the response because it is living, large-scale, centralised, and designed to become a practical knowledge base rather than a one-off static summary.

**Tone:** Measured, evidence-grounded, not dramatic. The problem is structural, not scandalous.

**Surveillance-map paper boundary:** Scene-setting only. Do not reproduce its full results or let this section drift into a standalone surveillance-programme review. The paper frames the need; the living review is the response.

**By the end of this section the audience grasps:** The field has information, but not yet infrastructure.

---

### Section B  -  What FIFA GBI Phase 1 Is

**Purpose:** Shift from problem to response. The audience should come away seeing the living review as a designed system  -  not another conventional one-off publication.

**Speaking logic:**

1. State the review's inclusive scope: four football disciplines (association football, futsal, beach soccer, para football), all sexes, age groups, competitive levels, and languages. Prospective cohort studies and randomised trials reporting injury, illness, or mental health in competitive football settings.
2. Explain the living architecture. This is not a single systematic review with an expiry date. It is designed for structured extraction, quality control, and annual updates. The evidence base grows; the synthesis stays current.
3. Name the dissemination pathway briefly: a FIFA-hosted dashboard and a public report aligned with major tournament cycles. These are outputs of the review, not the subject of the talk. Mention them to show practical intent, then move on.
4. Anchor the novelty claim: FIFA GBI is novel not because it is larger than prior reviews, but because it was designed from the outset as living evidence infrastructure for the full ecology of football.
5. Make the value proposition explicit: this creates a standardised, centralised, continuously updated evidence base for the global game, and can be described as the biggest systematic review of its kind when that helps clarify scale.
6. Use the preferred value wording where useful: FIFA GBI turns scattered football health evidence into standardised, comparable, continuously updating evidence.
7. If modelling is mentioned, frame it carefully: a structured living system creates the conditions for stronger hierarchical / Bayesian inference in sparse settings, with explicit uncertainty, and can support estimation across sexes, ages, places, and, where appropriate, over time, but it does not replace the need for surveillance itself.

**Tone:** Confident but not promotional. This is a scientific architecture, not a product.

**Dashboard boundary:** One sentence, maximum. Do not show the dashboard here. Do not describe its features.

**By the end of this section the audience grasps:** The novelty lies in inclusive, continuously updated infrastructure  -  not study volume alone.

---

### Section C  -  Comparability and the Consensus Backbone

**Purpose:** Carry the methodological weight of the talk. The audience should leave this section convinced that the living review's value depends on whether the studies it absorbs can actually be compared.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Open with the core message: accumulating more studies will not, on its own, solve the problem. Better classification, exposure capture, and reporting discipline matter just as much.
2. Walk through the consensus lineage as a three-node sequence:
   - **Fuller et al. 2006**  -  established the football-specific baseline: injury definitions, severity, recurrence, exposure capture.
   - **Bahr et al. 2020 (IOC consensus / STROBE-SIIS)**  -  widened the lens to athlete health problems broadly, with explicit guidance on severity, burden, population description, and reporting standards.
   - **Waldén et al. 2023**  -  translated the broader IOC framework back into football practice, reinforcing player-level exposure capture and sport-specific diagnostic coding.
3. Connect the lineage to the living review: this consensus sequence is not background decoration. It is the backbone that makes extraction comparable and future synthesis defensible.
4. Name the priority emphasis areas without over-indexing on technical detail:
   - Population description: discipline, sex, age, level, setting
   - Exposure definition and measurement
   - The distinction between incidence and burden
   - Illness and mental-health visibility alongside injury
   - Reporting quality that supports interpretation
5. Land the section summary: a living review becomes useful only when the studies it absorbs can be interpreted through comparable definitions, exposure logic, and burden reporting frameworks.

**Tone:** Intellectually serious. This is the section that earns credibility with the academic portion of the audience. Keep it clear, not dense.

**Key spoken takeaway:** The field does not only need more studies. It needs studies that can enter a comparable living evidence system.

---

### Section D  -  The Preliminary Landscape

**Purpose:** Show what the living review is already capturing  -  where the evidence concentrates and where it thins out. This section must be explicitly marked as provisional throughout.

**Data-status caveat  -  introduce once, reinforce visually:**

At the top of this section, state clearly: the patterns shown here are drawn from the current extraction dataset, which remains under cleaning and harmonisation. They are directional signals, not final epidemiological results. All figures will be confirmed against the final analysis dataset before publication.

After that verbal introduction, a persistent "Preliminary data" footer label on every results-oriented slide handles ongoing signalling without repeated spoken disclaimers that break the flow.

**Speaking logic:**

1. **Discipline coverage.** The evidence base is overwhelmingly association football. Futsal, para football, and beach soccer appear but at much smaller scale. This is a gap the living review is designed to track over time.
2. **Sex distribution.** Male-coded studies materially outnumber female-coded studies. A known structural imbalance  -  but a living review makes it measurable and trackable rather than assumed.
3. **Level of play.** Professional and elite settings dominate. Amateur and youth representation is thinner. Because the living review captures level explicitly, this gap becomes visible rather than buried.
4. **Outcome availability.** Injury fields are far more populated than illness fields. Mental-health capture is sparser still  -  mirroring what the surveillance-map paper found about illness and mental-health capture lagging behind injury capture in existing programmes.
5. **Temporal distribution.** Reasonable representation from 2020-2024, indicating active research production and a live pipeline of eligible studies.
6. Close this section by reframing the gaps as the argument for a living system: these patterns show where the evidence concentrates today. A living review is the instrument that lets us track whether and how those gaps close with each annual update.

**Claims this section must not make:**

- Final pooled incidence or burden estimates
- Definitive comparisons between football disciplines
- Locked study counts
- Strong illness or mental-health prevalence conclusions
- Any suggestion that the provisional export equals the final analysis dataset

---

### Section E  -  Forward Direction and Practical Takeaway

**Purpose:** Close with forward momentum and a concrete call to action. The audience should leave knowing what comes next and what they themselves can do.

**Speaking logic:**

1. Name the forward path briefly:
   - Annual living-review updates
   - A FIFA-hosted dashboard as a dissemination output
   - A public report aligned with major tournament cycles
   - An evidence system designed for use by clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers
2. Deliver the practical takeaway directly:

   > **If you run a surveillance programme, design a cohort study, or report football injury and illness data  -  consensus-aligned definitions and complete reporting are what allow your work to enter a living evidence system. The field needs your data, but it needs your data to be comparable.**

3. Reinforce what the audience can act on:
   - Design surveillance studies so they can be compared across contexts
   - Report exposure, definitions, burden, and population characteristics clearly
   - Resist the assumption that evidence from professional men's 11-a-side football generalises to the whole sport
   - Treat living synthesis as part of player-health infrastructure, not an academic abstraction

**Tone:** Direct, collegial, forward-looking. Not a sales pitch. Not a plea. A professional statement of shared responsibility.

## 4. Visual Direction

### Design Language

- **Background:** White or very light neutral  -  off-white, warm grey at most.
- **Text:** Dark grey or near-black for body text. High contrast throughout.
- **Accent colour:** FIFA blue as the primary accent, used sparingly  -  headings, chart fills, key-sentence highlights.
- **Typography:** Large, confident type for key claims. Sans-serif throughout. Minimum readable body size on screen: 24pt equivalent.
- **One idea per slide.** No composite layouts with competing visuals.
- **Academic keynote aesthetic.** Clean, restrained, high signal-to-noise. No gradients, drop shadows, or decorative elements.

### Chart and Data Visual Rules

- Bar charts: horizontal, single-colour fill (FIFA blue), labelled directly. Avoid legends where possible.
- Proportional charts: waffle or area  -  not pie charts.
- Every provisional-data slide carries a persistent "Preliminary data" footer label in a muted but readable weight.
- No decimals on any provisional figure. Round to whole numbers or use qualitative descriptors ("materially outnumber," "much smaller representation").
- No confidence intervals, p-values, or forest plots. This is a descriptive landscape overview, not a meta-analysis.

### What Not To Show

- No screenshots of the extraction tool or dashboard UI in the main talk body.
- No software architecture diagrams.
- No Phase 2 or Phase 3 material.
- No dashboard walkthrough slides.

## 5. Remotion Scene Opportunities

Each scene serves progressive reveal and structural clarity  -  not decoration. Motion should slow for key claims and pick up slightly during transitional passages.

### Scene 1  -  Fragmentation to System (Section A → B transition)

Scattered elements  -  study icons, discipline labels, population tags  -  float on screen in a loose, unstructured composition. On cue, they animate into a structured arrangement: the living-review architecture diagram. The transition takes 3-4 seconds.

**Message:** The project brings order to a fragmented evidence base.
**Technique:** Opacity fade-in for scattered elements, then coordinated translate and scale to final positions. Ease-out timing.

### Scene 2  -  Global but Uneven (Slide A2)

A world map or distributed-node composition. Dots animate in by region, staggered over 2-3 seconds. Concentration in certain areas becomes immediately visible; blank zones stay empty.

**Message:** Football is global, but surveillance and evidence are not evenly distributed.
**Technique:** Staggered opacity + scale-up per dot, grouped by region. No bounce or overshoot  -  clean appear.

### Scene 3  -  Scope Expansion (Slide B1)

The four football discipline icons appear in sequence (association football, futsal, beach soccer, para football). Once all four are visible, subgroup dimensions  -  sex, age, level, language  -  radiate outward from the centre as labelled lines or nodes.

**Message:** Novelty comes from inclusive scope, not study volume alone.
**Technique:** Sequential fade-in for disciplines (0.5s stagger), then radial draw-on for subgroup dimensions.

### Scene 4  -  Consensus as Backbone (Slide C2)

Three timeline nodes (2006, 2020, 2023) appear in sequence. Each node expands briefly to reveal its one-line contribution, then contracts to its compact label. The timeline line draws on left-to-right, connecting them.

**Message:** Comparability rests on a deliberate scientific lineage.
**Technique:** Draw-on line, sequential node expand/contract, text fade-in.

### Scene 5  -  Preliminary Pattern Reveal (Slides D2-D5)

Charts build sequentially within each slide. Bars draw on from left to right (horizontal bar charts) or bottom to top. Fills appear progressively rather than all at once. The "Preliminary data" footer fades in once at D1 and persists from there.

**Message:** Even at this stage, the current landscape already shows where evidence concentrates and where it thins out.
**Technique:** Draw-on bar fills with staggered timing. Opacity for labels. No morphing between charts  -  each chart is its own composition.

### Scene 6  -  Living Update Loop (Slide B2)

The circular process diagram (search → extract → validate → update → disseminate) animates as a draw-on loop. Each node highlights in sequence with a brief label appear. The loop completes and pulses once to reinforce the recurring nature.

**Message:** This is recurring infrastructure, not a static publication.
**Technique:** Circular path draw-on, sequential node highlight, single pulse at completion.

### Scene 7  -  End-State Utility (Slide E1)

Dashboard and report outputs appear last in the roadmap, with a quiet sequential fade-in. They arrive after the annual-update node  -  reinforcing that dissemination follows the science.

**Message:** Dissemination follows the science; it does not replace it.
**Technique:** Sequential fade-in, 0.8s stagger, no emphasis animation. Understated by design.

### Animation Principles (All Scenes)

- Transitions: horizontal wipes, opacity fades, draw-on effects. No 3D, no bounces, no rapid morphs.
- Tempo: slower for key claims (Scenes 1, 4), moderate for data (Scene 5), quicker for structural or transitional moments (Scenes 6, 7).
- No animation should imply stronger inference than the data supports. Charts build  -  they do not transform.

## 6. Provisional Data Language Guide

### Use verbatim or in close paraphrase during Section D:

- "These are preliminary extraction patterns from the current dataset."
- "The dataset remains under cleaning and harmonisation."
- "These are directional signals rather than final epidemiological estimates."
- "All exact figures will need confirmation against the final analysis dataset."
- "These patterns will be confirmed against the final analysis dataset."

### Avoid:

- "We found that…" (implies completed analysis)
- "The evidence shows…" (implies synthesis-level confidence)
- "X% of studies report…" (implies locked denominators)
- Any locked percentage, decimal, confidence interval, or pooled claim not yet validated

## 7. Closing Takeaway and Audience Value

### What the audience should remember

Three things, in priority order:

1. **The problem is real and current.** Football epidemiology evidence is fragmented across disciplines, populations, and reporting standards. Existing surveillance is globally uneven.

2. **FIFA GBI is building a living system, not publishing a single paper.** The living review architecture  -  consensus-aligned extraction, structured quality control, annual updates, accessible dissemination  -  is designed to keep the evidence base current and comparable.

3. **Their own work can contribute directly.** If they collect or report football health data, consensus-aligned definitions and complete reporting are what allow that work to enter a living evidence system. The field needs comparable data, not just more of it.

### Five gains the audience takes away

By the end of the talk, the audience should leave with:

- A sharper understanding of why football epidemiology still struggles with fragmentation
- A clear picture of what the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to do
- A practical appreciation of why consensus-aligned definitions and reporting matter
- An early view of where the current evidence base concentrates or thins out, presented with appropriate caution
- A direct implication for their own work: surveillance and cohort studies become more useful when designed to be comparable and capable of entering a living evidence system

### The single closing sentence

> **The field needs your data, but it needs your data to be comparable.**

This sentence should appear as a full-slide typographic treatment on the final content slide before acknowledgments. It is the line the audience takes home.

## Final Recommendation

Build the talk as a scientific argument, not a project update. Open with fragmentation, use the surveillance-map paper to establish urgency, introduce FIFA GBI Phase 1 as living evidence infrastructure, show why consensus alignment makes that infrastructure credible, and let provisional descriptive patterns illustrate the current shape of the evidence base  -  nothing more.

If that order holds, the talk will land as rigorous, timely, and useful. Not because it presents a project, but because it presents a different way of organising football player-health evidence at global scale. The dashboard stays downstream of the Living Systematic Review, exactly where it belongs: proof of practical intent, not the story itself.
