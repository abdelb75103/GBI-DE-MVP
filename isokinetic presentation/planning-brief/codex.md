# Master Planning Brief  
## Academic Conference Talk on the FIFA Global Burden of Injury and Illness in Football Living Systematic Review

## Purpose

This brief sets the narrative, structural, and visual logic for a conference talk focused only on Phase 1 of the FIFA Global Burden of Injury and Illness in Football: the Living Systematic Review. It is designed to guide later slide writing, visual design, and narration.

## Assumptions

- The talk is for an academic or clinically informed sports-medicine audience.
- The project documents are the source of truth for scope, emphasis, and boundaries.
- The current export can support directional descriptive signals only and must not be treated as final epidemiological analysis.
- The talk should present the dashboard as one dissemination output of the living review, not as the main story.

---

## 1. Recommended Core Narrative

## Strongest Narrative Choice
**Football does not only need more player-health data; it needs living, comparable, globally inclusive evidence infrastructure.**

This is the strongest framing because it does four things at once:

- gives the audience a clear problem before introducing the project
- makes the review feel necessary rather than merely ambitious
- positions FIFA GBI as infrastructure, not just another review
- creates room for preliminary descriptive findings without forcing premature claims

## One-Sentence Thesis
Football is a global sport, but its injury and illness evidence base remains fragmented across disciplines, populations, settings, and reporting standards; the FIFA GBI Living Systematic Review is designed to turn that fragmented literature into a continuously updated, consensus-aligned evidence system.

## Why This Narrative Works
It aligns with the project scope documents, the PROSPERO-style review logic, and the 2026 surveillance-map paper. It also gives the talk a memorable intellectual arc:

1. the problem is global fragmentation  
2. the consequence is weak comparability and incomplete burden visibility  
3. the solution is a living systematic review built for breadth, rigor, and updating  

That arc is stronger than a project-summary talk, a methods-only talk, or a dashboard-led talk.

---

## 2. How To Introduce FIFA GBI

## Opening Problem Statement
The talk should open with the mismatch between the scale of football and the unevenness of its player-health evidence base.

Key opening ideas:

- football is global, but its epidemiology evidence is not yet globally integrated
- prior reviews have often been narrow, static, and skewed toward professional male 11-a-side cohorts
- meaningful parts of football remain underrepresented, including futsal, beach soccer, para football, women’s football, younger cohorts, and non-English evidence
- static reviews age quickly in a fast-moving surveillance field

## Best Framing Role for the 2026 Surveillance-Map Paper
Use the 2026 paper as the clearest “why now” reference.

Its function in the talk is not to review surveillance programmes in detail. Its function is to establish that:

- football already has surveillance activity
- that activity is unevenly distributed
- men’s settings are better covered than women’s
- injury surveillance is more common than illness or mental-health capture
- consensus-based practice is not universal

This helps the audience see that the field is active but still patchy. That is the exact conditions under which a living synthesis system becomes valuable.

## How To Describe What Makes FIFA GBI Novel
The novelty should be framed as a combination of scope, method, and continuity:

- **Scope:** association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football
- **Population breadth:** all sexes, age groups, levels of play, and languages
- **Outcome breadth:** injury, illness, and mental-health-relevant outcomes within competitive football settings
- **Methodological discipline:** structured extraction, quality control, and consensus-aligned coding/reporting
- **Living design:** annual updating rather than a one-off static synthesis
- **Translation pathway:** FIFA-hosted dissemination outputs, including a future dashboard

A strong formulation is:

**FIFA GBI is novel not because it is larger than prior reviews, but because it is designed as living evidence infrastructure for the full ecology of football.**

## 3. Recommended Talk Architecture

## Overall Balance
The talk should balance three layers:

- **Project introduction and need:** about 40%
- **Preliminary descriptive signals:** about 30%
- **Consensus and comparability story:** about 30%

This keeps the talk grounded in a scientific problem, gives the audience something concrete to see, and ends with practical methodological value rather than a vague future promise.

## Recommended Flow

### Section 1: Why football needs a living review
Purpose:
Establish the global evidence problem.

Content:
- football’s scale and diversity
- fragmentation across discipline, sex, age, level, language, and outcome reporting
- the limits of static reviews
- surveillance-map paper as scene-setting evidence of uneven coverage

### Section 2: What FIFA GBI Phase 1 is
Purpose:
Define the living review clearly and credibly.

Content:
- Phase 1 only
- in-scope disciplines and populations
- study types and settings in scope
- structured searches, extraction, and quality control
- annual updating logic
- living review as infrastructure rather than just manuscript production

### Section 3: Why comparability is the scientific hinge
Purpose:
Show that breadth alone is not enough.

Content:
- importance of consensus-aligned definitions and reporting
- 2006 football consensus as foundational
- 2020 IOC consensus as broader athlete-health reporting logic
- 2023 football-specific extension as practical bridge back to football
- comparability as the condition for subgroup synthesis and future burden estimation

### Section 4: What the current provisional landscape already suggests
Purpose:
Offer preliminary descriptive value without overclaiming.

Content:
- evidence is still concentrated in association football
- male-coded and elite/professional settings dominate visible extracted material
- injury reporting is much more common than illness reporting
- structured variables already support subgroup and reporting-quality summaries
- these are planning signals, not final burden estimates

### Section 5: What the audience should take forward
Purpose:
End with a practical and memorable message.

Content:
- global football needs better connected evidence, not isolated studies
- future work should improve coverage and comparability together
- the living review is meant to support clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers
- FIFA-hosted dissemination outputs matter because evidence should be usable, not only publishable

## 4. How To Use Provisional Findings Without Overclaiming

## Recommended Framing Language
The results section should be explicitly labeled with terms such as:

- **preliminary descriptive signals**
- **current extracted landscape**
- **directional patterns from the provisional export**
- **planning-stage observations pending dataset cleaning and harmonization**

## Safe Claims
The talk can safely show that the current extracted landscape appears to be:

- dominated by association football
- weighted toward male and professional/elite settings
- much richer in injury fields than illness fields
- variable in reporting completeness across outcomes and subgroups

These are not epidemiological conclusions. They are evidence-base structure signals.

## Unsafe Claims
The talk should not present:

- final pooled incidence or burden estimates
- definitive comparisons between football disciplines
- locked counts that have not been rechecked after cleaning
- strong illness or mental-health prevalence conclusions from the current export
- any implication that the export already represents the final analysis dataset

## Best Practice for the Results Slides
Each results-oriented slide should visually and verbally signal caution:

- put “Preliminary” in the slide subtitle
- use “current extracted records/studies” rather than “global truth”
- emphasize distribution, coverage, completeness, and feasibility
- avoid decimals, confidence language, or synthesis phrasing that implies final meta-analysis

The aim is to demonstrate value and direction, not closure.

## 5. Method and Comparability Story

## Methodological Spine
The methodological story should be concise but serious. It should make the audience trust the review design without bogging the talk down.

Priority points:

- searches across major biomedical and sport databases
- structured, codebook-based extraction
- pilot testing and quality control
- subset double extraction and source cross-validation
- coding aligned with IOC 2020 and the 2023 football-specific extension
- long-run Bayesian hierarchical synthesis plan to handle heterogeneity and sparse subgroups
- annual updating to prevent rapid obsolescence

## How To Position Consensus Sources
The consensus papers should not appear as a literature detour. They should appear as the scientific backbone that makes a living review meaningful.

Use a message like:

**A living review only becomes useful if the studies it absorbs can be interpreted through comparable definitions, exposure logic, and burden reporting frameworks.**

That is the bridge between methods and significance.

## Recommended Methodological Emphasis
Do not over-index on technical detail. Emphasize:

- population description
- exposure definition
- injury versus burden distinction
- illness visibility
- football-specific reporting discipline

The audience should leave understanding that comparability is not a side issue. It is the condition for interpretable synthesis.

## 6. Visual Strategy

## Visual Tone
The deck should feel:

- academic
- clean
- authoritative
- globally minded
- modern but restrained

Avoid product-demo aesthetics. Avoid overly dense spreadsheet visuals. The deck should look like a scientific keynote, not a software launch.

## Recommended Visual System
Use a limited visual vocabulary:

- clean typographic slides with one dominant idea each
- global map or world-linked structure early
- disciplined charts for provisional landscape signals
- simple consensus lineage or evidence-flow diagrams
- restrained FIFA-adjacent visual seriousness without branding overload

## Best Visual Types By Section

### Opening
- global football imagery abstracted into a world network or evidence map
- one slide showing fragmentation across discipline, sex, age, level, and language

### Project Definition
- layered scope diagram showing association football, futsal, beach soccer, para football
- inclusion matrix across sex, age, level, and language
- living review cycle diagram showing search, extraction, quality control, update, dissemination

### Comparability Story
- concise timeline or lineage visual:
  - 2006 football consensus
  - 2020 IOC consensus
  - 2023 football-specific extension
- “from fragmented reporting to comparable evidence” diagram

### Preliminary Results
- bar chart or segmented distribution showing discipline imbalance
- simple split chart for male/female/mixed coding
- reporting-completeness chart contrasting injury versus illness field availability
- possibly a matrix heatmap for coverage by subgroup type, if kept highly legible

### Ending
- one dissemination slide showing report and dashboard as outputs, secondary to the review itself
- final takeaway slide with a single strong sentence

## 7. Remotion-Friendly Animation Beats

## Animation Principles
The motion should clarify structure and build memory, not decorate the deck.

Use:

- progressive reveal
- grouping and regrouping
- disciplined motion tied to argument shifts
- clean tempo changes between problem, method, and signal slides

Avoid:

- flashy transitions
- rapid chart morphs that imply stronger inference than exists
- dashboard-like interface animations dominating the scientific story

## Recommended Animation Beats

### Beat 1: Fragmentation to System
Start with scattered elements representing studies, disciplines, and populations.
Animate them into a coherent living-review structure.
Meaning: the project brings order to a fragmented evidence base.

### Beat 2: Global but Uneven
Use a subtle map or distributed-node composition.
Highlight concentration rather than universal coverage.
Meaning: football is global, surveillance and evidence are not evenly global.

### Beat 3: Scope Expansion
Reveal the four football disciplines one by one, then overlay sex, age, level, and language.
Meaning: novelty comes from inclusive scope, not just study volume.

### Beat 4: Consensus as Backbone
Animate the three consensus milestones as a spine or scaffold.
Meaning: comparability is constructed, not assumed.

### Beat 5: Preliminary Pattern Reveal
Introduce provisional charts sequentially:
- discipline concentration
- sex/setting imbalance
- injury versus illness reporting asymmetry
Meaning: the current landscape can already show gaps and direction.

### Beat 6: Living Update Loop
Animate a circular or cyclical process:
search -> extract -> validate -> update -> disseminate
Meaning: this is recurring infrastructure, not a static publication.

### Beat 7: End-State Utility
Quietly reveal report/dashboard outputs last.
Meaning: dissemination follows the science; it does not replace it.

## 8. Recommended Slide-Level Emphasis

## Slides That Should Carry the Talk
If the deck is short, these are the non-negotiable moments:

1. The problem slide: global sport, fragmented evidence  
2. The “why now” slide using the 2026 surveillance-map paper  
3. The “what FIFA GBI is” slide  
4. The comparability/consensus slide  
5. The preliminary landscape slide  
6. The living-review workflow slide  
7. The takeaway slide  

## Slides That Should Stay Secondary
- detailed search syntax
- deep extraction workflow mechanics
- dashboard feature walkthrough
- broader downstream phases of the project
- too many discipline-specific examples unless needed for one clean illustrative point

## 9. The Practical Takeaway

## What the Audience Should Leave With
The audience should leave with one practical conclusion:

**If football wants credible global estimates of injury and illness burden, it must invest in evidence systems that are inclusive, continuously updated, and methodologically comparable.**

## More Applied Version for Clinicians and Researchers
A second-level takeaway can be:

- design surveillance studies so they can be compared later
- report exposure, definitions, burden, and population characteristics clearly
- do not assume that evidence from professional men’s 11-a-side football generalizes to the whole sport
- treat living synthesis as part of player-health infrastructure

That gives the talk immediate professional value without drifting into a policy speech.

---

## 10. Guardrails For the Later Talk Build

## Keep In
- Phase 1 only
- global need
- inclusive football scope
- living-review logic
- consensus-aligned comparability
- provisional descriptive signals with clear caveats
- dashboard/report as dissemination outputs only

## Keep Out
- Phase 2 and Phase 3 in the main talk body
- any product-demo framing
- any claim that the provisional export equals the final cleaned dataset
- any final burden estimates not validated against the cleaned analysis file
- any structure that makes the talk feel like a methods appendix

---

## Final Recommendation

The talk should be built around a clear scientific claim: **football needs living evidence infrastructure because its current player-health evidence is globally important but unevenly captured, unevenly reported, and not yet sufficiently comparable.** FIFA GBI should be introduced as the first serious attempt to build that infrastructure across the full football ecosystem.

The preliminary results should serve that argument, not compete with it. They should show that the evidence base is still concentrated, incomplete, and structurally uneven. The consensus story should explain why that matters scientifically. The dashboard should appear late, briefly, and only as proof that this living review is designed for use.

If executed well, the talk will feel rigorous, timely, and memorable because it does not merely present a project. It presents a new way to organise football player-health evidence at global scale.
