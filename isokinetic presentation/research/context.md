```text
# Context

## Project Framing

The talk is about the FIFA Global Burden of Injury and Illness in Football project as a Living Systematic Review.

The opening should explain:

- football is global, but the epidemiology evidence base is fragmented
- most previous reviews are narrow, static, and often centred on professional male 11-a-side football
- FIFA GBI is building a living, continuously updated evidence system across football disciplines, sexes, ages, levels of play, and languages

## What Must Be Included

- FIFA GBI project introduction
- why a living systematic review is needed
- novelty of including association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football
- multilingual and globally inclusive ambition
- the future FIFA-hosted dashboard as one dissemination output
- provisional descriptive findings from the current extraction dataset
- consensus-statement alignment and best-practice reporting logic
- a practical takeaway for the audience

## What Must Not Happen

- do not turn the talk into a dashboard product demo
- do not center Phase 2 or Phase 3
- do not treat the current CSV export as the final cleaned analysis sheet
- do not overclaim pooled epidemiological results from the provisional export

## Data Caveat

The current export is still being cleaned, standardised, and expanded. It is useful for identifying what descriptive signals exist, but any exact counts or claims used in the final talk must later be rechecked against the cleaned analysis dataset.

## Framing Reference

The paper `Player health surveillance in football: a global map of programmes in national and international leagues and tournaments` is a mandatory framing reference. Use it to guide tone, structure, and how global surveillance is described, but do not copy its structure.
```

```text
# Project Notes

## What the FIFA GBI Living Review Is Trying to Do

- Build a living systematic review of injury and illness in football
- Cover association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football
- Include all sexes, age groups, levels of play, and languages
- Move beyond static one-off reviews that become outdated quickly
- Support FIFA, clinicians, researchers, and decision-makers with a continuously updated evidence base

## Core Methodological Ideas from the Project Documents

- structured data extraction and quality control
- consensus-aligned coding and reporting
- Bayesian hierarchical synthesis to handle heterogeneity and sparse subgroups
- annual living-review updates
- a FIFA-hosted dashboard as one dissemination route

## Important Talk Implications

- the talk should introduce the living review as infrastructure, not just a paper
- the talk should explain why football epidemiology needs this kind of system
- preliminary findings should be shown as directional outputs of a larger program of work
- comparability and consensus alignment matter, but they should support the main story rather than replace it
```

```text
# Derived Scope Notes

This file is a text-only synthesis of the project-scope documents referenced in `sources.md`.

Primary source documents reviewed:

- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Desktop/FIFA/FIFA GBI Project Plan .docx`
- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Desktop/FIFA/UCD Project Plan - FIFA Living Systematic Review .docx`
- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Desktop/FIFA/FIFA GBI Prospero Proctocol.docx`
- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Desktop/FIFA/A.BABIKER Abstract Isokinetic 2026.docx`
- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Desktop/FIFA/FIFA GBI - Meeting Summary (27 January).docx`

## Source-of-Truth Framing

- The talk should focus on Phase 1 only: the living systematic review.
- The project exists because football epidemiology evidence is fragmented, outdated, and still skewed toward professional male 11-a-side cohorts.
- FIFA GBI is meant to become a living evidence infrastructure, not just a one-off review paper.
- The dashboard is an output and dissemination route of the living review, not the core subject of the talk.

## Phase 1 Scope

- Include association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football.
- Include all sexes, age groups, competitive levels, and languages.
- Include prospective cohort studies and randomized trials that report injury, illness, or mental health outcomes in competitive football settings.
- Tournament and league settings are in scope.
- Referee studies may be included but should be reported separately.

## Method Signals To Preserve In The Talk

- Searches are built around MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, and SportsDiscus, with reference-list screening from relevant reviews.
- Data extraction is structured, codebook-based, and aligned with the IOC 2020 consensus plus the 2023 football-specific extension.
- Quality control includes pilot testing, subset double extraction, and cross-validation against source papers.
- The long-run synthesis plan is Bayesian hierarchical/meta-analytic so sparse subgroups can still be modelled while respecting heterogeneity.
- Annual living-review updates are planned, with a longer cadence for larger systematic refreshes.

## Content That Supports The Conference Narrative

- The review aims to estimate injury incidence and burden across disciplines, sexes, ages, and levels of play.
- High-profile diagnosis examples repeatedly named in the project material are concussion, ACL injury, and hamstring strain.
- A major justification for the living approach is that static reviews age quickly and leave newer surveillance studies unintegrated.
- Consensus-aligned definitions and reporting are treated as essential for comparability across studies, not as a side issue.

## Important Boundaries

- Do not center Phase 2 or Phase 3 in the talk body.
- Do not overplay the dashboard or the extraction tool as if this were a product presentation.
- Do not present uncleaned export counts as final epidemiological truth.
- Any discussion of direct data from large surveillance systems would require protocol updates and should be framed carefully.

## Practical Output Direction

- The current conference framing should emphasize global need, methodological rigor, inclusive scope, and the value of a living evidence system.
- The talk can mention a FIFA-hosted report and dashboard as dissemination outputs.
- The January 27 leadership summary suggests a public report aligned with the World Cup timeline, with academic papers following later.
```

```text
# Provisional Data Signals

This note summarizes directional signals from:

- `/Users/abdelbabiker/Downloads/GBI-DE-MVP-main/exports/all-studies-export-2026-04-03T20-52-31-100Z.csv`

These are planning signals only. They must be rechecked against the cleaned analysis dataset before any final talk claims are locked.

## Snapshot On April 3, 2026

- The export contains 1,014 rows and 570 paper IDs.
- Status labels are mixed, including `extracted` (740 rows), `american_data` (81), `flagged` (71), `systematic_review` (58), and `uefa` (40).
- Core metadata are still incomplete in part of the export. For example, 822 rows have populated title and lead-author fields, while many others remain partially blank.

## Directional Signals Safe To Use For Planning

- The current populated discipline rows are dominated by association football:
  - 712 association-football rows
  - 42 futsal or indoor rows
  - 9 para-football or 5-a-side rows
  - 4 beach-soccer rows
- Male-coded rows materially outnumber female-coded rows in the raw labels:
  - 541 male rows
  - 117 female rows
  - additional mixed or combined-sex labels are present
- Professional or elite settings dominate the populated level-of-play labels, with smaller amateur and youth/college representation.
- Injury measures are much more available than illness measures in the current export:
  - 264 paper IDs have a populated `injuryIncidenceOverall`
  - 38 paper IDs have a populated `injuryBurden`
  - 8 paper IDs have a populated `illnessIncidenceOverall`
- Recent publication years are reasonably represented in the populated metadata, especially from 2020 through 2024.

## What These Signals Support In The Talk

- A coverage-gap message: the evidence base is still heavily centred on association football and male/professional settings.
- A comparability message: even within the current export, reporting completeness varies substantially across outcomes and subgroups.
- A feasibility message: the project is already capturing the fields needed for subgroup summaries, reporting-quality discussions, and future dashboard filters.

## What These Signals Do Not Support

- Final pooled incidence or burden claims.
- Definitive comparisons between football disciplines.
- Locked prevalence or illness conclusions.
- Exact study counts for the final conference deck without rechecking the cleaned analysis dataset.
```
