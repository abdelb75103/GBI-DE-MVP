## Source-of-Truth Framing

- The talk should focus on Phase 1 only: the living systematic review.
- The project exists because football epidemiology evidence is fragmented, outdated, and still skewed toward professional male 11-a-side cohorts.
- FIFA GBI is meant to become a living evidence infrastructure, not just a one-off review paper.
- The dashboard is an output and dissemination route of the living review, not the core subject of the talk.

## Phase 1 Scope

- Include association football, futsal, beach soccer, and para football.
- Include all sexes, age groups, competitive levels, and languages.
- Include prospective cohort studies and randomized trials that report injury, illness, or mental health outcomes in competitive football settings.
- Tournament and league settings are in scope.
- Referee studies may be included but should be reported separately.

## Method Signals To Preserve In The Talk

- Searches are built around MEDLINE, PubMed, Embase, and SportsDiscus, with reference-list screening from relevant reviews.
- Data extraction is structured, codebook-based, and aligned with the IOC 2020 consensus plus the 2023 football-specific extension.
- Quality control includes pilot testing, subset double extraction, and cross-validation against source papers.
- The long-run synthesis plan is Bayesian hierarchical/meta-analytic so sparse subgroups can still be modelled while respecting heterogeneity.
- Annual living-review updates are planned, with a longer cadence for larger systematic refreshes.

## Content That Supports The Conference Narrative

- The review aims to estimate injury incidence and burden across disciplines, sexes, ages, and levels of play.
- High-profile diagnosis examples repeatedly named in the project material are concussion, ACL injury, and hamstring strain.
- A major justification for the living approach is that static reviews age quickly and leave newer surveillance studies unintegrated.
- Consensus-aligned definitions and reporting are treated as essential for comparability across studies, not as a side issue.

## Important Boundaries

- Do not center Phase 2 or Phase 3 in the talk body.
- Do not overplay the dashboard or the extraction tool as if this were a product presentation.
- Do not present uncleaned export counts as final epidemiological truth.
- Any discussion of direct data from large surveillance systems would require protocol updates and should be framed carefully.

## Practical Output Direction

- The current conference framing should emphasize global need, methodological rigor, inclusive scope, and the value of a living evidence system.
- The talk can mention a FIFA-hosted report and dashboard as dissemination outputs.
- The January 27 leadership summary suggests a public report aligned with the World Cup timeline, with academic papers following later.
