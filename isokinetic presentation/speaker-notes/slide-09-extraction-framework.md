# Slide: 570 Papers, One Common Language

## Current Sequence Position
Immediately after the extraction breakdown slide.

## Purpose
Shift the audience from counts of paper types to the logic of the extraction system itself. The point of the slide is not database size. The point is that every paper is read against the same consensus-aligned framework, which makes missingness and comparability visible.

## Speaking Cues & Reveal Order

* **Headline appears immediately**
  * **Say:** "Once the papers are in extraction, the next question is not just how many there are. It's whether they can be read in a standardised way."

* **`448` counter appears**
  * **Action:** Click/wait for the counter to build from 1 to 448.
  * **Say:** "For each paper, we have up to 448 possible extraction fields. That covers much more than just injury counts."

* **`570` papers appears**
  * **Action:** Click/wait for the second number.
  * **Say:** "And we are applying that same structure across 570 papers."

* **Total possible points appears**
  * **Action:** Click/wait for the total to appear.
  * **Say:** "That creates the possibility of more than 255,000 paper-level data points across the extraction system."

* **Final takeaway appears**
  * **Action:** Click/wait for the closing line.
  * **Say:** "Now, that does not mean every study reports every field. In fact, many do not. But that is exactly why this matters. Because when every paper is extracted against the same framework, the reporting gaps become visible rather than hidden."

## Key Points To Cover Verbally
- The extraction framework follows the consensus-aligned logic used across the project.
- The extraction system spans injury, illness, exposure, burden, severity, recurrence, mechanism, and mental health dimensions.
- Many papers will leave large parts of the framework empty.
- That is not a flaw in the slide. It is part of the scientific value:
  - it shows where the literature is rich
  - it shows where it is thin
  - it shows where reporting is inconsistent
- The framework turns a pile of papers into comparable structured evidence.

## Short Optional Line
"The point is not that every paper gives 448 answers. The point is that every paper is asked the same questions."
