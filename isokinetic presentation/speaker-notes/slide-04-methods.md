## Purpose
Reintroduce the structured review pipeline before the Bayesian explanation. This slide should show that FIFA GBI is not a loose collection exercise but a disciplined system built around search, screening, extraction, validation, modelling, and updating.

## Speaking Cues & Reveal Order

* **Base shell, headline, and support line appear immediately:**
  * **Say:** "This is not just a review in name. It is built as a system."
* **Pipeline appears node by node:**
  * **Action:** Click through `Search`, `Screen`, `Extract`, `Validate`, `Model`, `Update`.
  * **Say:** "We search systematically, screen consistently, extract in a structured way, validate the evidence, model it, and then update the system as new studies enter."
* **Bottom cards appear one by one:**
  * **Action:** Click/wait for each card.
  * **Say:** "That structure gives us three things: it lets us borrow strength across sparse subgroups, it supports subgroup estimation with updateable evidence, and it keeps uncertainty explicit rather than hidden."

## Key Message
- Structured review methods make the evidence base consistent and comparable.
- Modelling sits inside the review system, not outside it.
- The living update model is part of the method, not an afterthought.
