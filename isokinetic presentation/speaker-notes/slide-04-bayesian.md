## Purpose
Introduce the overarching method behind the statistical modelling. Explain it simply without statistics jargon, framing it as a living evidence system that updates as new evidence arrives. Emphasise that it handles sparse settings well by explicitly modelling uncertainty.

## Key Bayesian Explanation
- Bayesian models combine **prior understanding** with **new data** to produce an **updated posterior**.
- Especially useful in **sparse groups** or uneven evidence settings.
- The approach can **borrow strength** across related subgroups.
- **Uncertainty is made visible**, especially where evidence is sparse.
- Helps make better use of **incomplete and heterogeneous evidence**.
